Formal education has built-in credibility signals

4th May 2020 at 8:25pm

One advantage of formal education is that it comes bundled with credibility signalling. There is a certificate to prove that you completed the course to a certain standard.

If the institution is exclusive then the mere fact that you were accepted gives clues to your ability.

The fact that you completed the course indicates your persistence, dedication and commitment to the subject area.

Whatever trust and belief one has in the course, passes on to the people who complete the course.

Self-directed learning lacks these "signals" so even if it yields better learning outcomes, it could still prejudice you in the job market.

Self-directed learners can also generate credibility signals but they need to be more pro-active about this, for example by building something that demonstrates their ability, assembling a portfolio or learning in public. See Learning in public gives credibility and proof of your self-directed learning.
